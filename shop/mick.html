Mick Thomson

Iconic guitarist and one of the founding members of the heavy metal band Slipknot, known for his intense riffs and masked persona.

About the Legend

☛ Mick Thomson was born on November 3, 1973, in Des Moines, Iowa, USA.

☛ He is the lead guitarist of Slipknot, the Grammy-winning heavy metal band known for its aggressive sound and theatrical performances.

☛ Mick joined Slipknot in 1996 and is recognized as "#7" in the band's lineup.

☛ Known for his powerful, intricate guitar playing, he has contributed to some of the band’s most iconic albums, including "Iowa," "Vol. 3: (The Subliminal Verses)," and ".5: The Gray Chapter."

☛ Mick's signature guitar models, including those by Ibanez and Jackson, are sought after by metal guitarists worldwide.

☛ Outside of music, Mick is known for his enigmatic personality and dedication to the craft of guitar playing.

☛ Slipknot's unique blend of music, Mick’s guitar work, and the band’s visual aesthetics have made them one of the most influential bands in the metal genre.

☛ Mick remains a key figure in the band, contributing to their continuing evolution and global success.
